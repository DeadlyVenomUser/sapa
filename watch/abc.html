The Story

A Grief, A Secret,
& One Remarkable Octopus

Tova Sullivan has spent her evenings mopping floors at the Sowell Bay Aquarium, finding solace in the quiet company of Marcellus — a giant Pacific octopus with an uncanny ability to observe the humans around him. When a young man named Cameron starts searching for answers about his birth mother, their lives intersect in ways neither could have predicted.

Told in alternating voices — the determined, wryly funny Marcellus and the deeply human characters whose lives he quietly observes — this is a story about the connections we forge, the secrets we keep, and the extraordinary ways in which the universe conspires to bring us home. Funny, deeply moving, and full of wonder, it asks: what do we owe each other, across every imaginable boundary?
